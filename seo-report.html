Quick Website Audit

OK Irrigation — 6 things holding the site back on Google

Prepared for okirrigation.com · checked live on the current site

Good news first: the site loads, the phone number is right, and the reviews are strong. These are the fixable gaps stopping it from showing up when people search for a sprinkler company nearby — roughly in priority order.

High

1 · No meta description

This is the grey text that shows under your name in Google results. Right now Google is guessing it. A written one is your free ad copy on the results page — it's what makes someone click you instead of the next company.

Found: no <meta name="description"> tag on the homepage
High

2 · The page title is just "OK Irrigation"

The title is the blue clickable headline in Google — and it's one of the biggest ranking signals. Bare company name wastes it. It should read something like "Sprinkler Installation & Repair in Minneapolis | OK Irrigation." That single line tells Google what you do and where.

Found: <title>OK Irrigation</title> — no service, no city
High

3 · No location or service keywords in the words on the page

People search "sprinkler install Minneapolis" or "irrigation repair Chanhassen." Those exact phrases barely appear on the site, so Google has little reason to match you to them. Naming your services and the towns you cover, in plain sentences, is what gets you found locally.

Medium

4 · No business "schema" (structured data)

This is hidden code that hands Google your name, phone, hours, service area, and star rating in a format it trusts — and can unlock the star ratings that show right in search results. The current site has none.

Found: no LocalBusiness JSON-LD on the page
Medium

5 · The headline says "Welcome to OK Irrigation"

Your main headline is prime real estate for both Google and visitors. "Welcome" says nothing. Something like "Sprinkler Installation & Repair Across the Twin Cities" works harder on both fronts.

Quick

6 · That strong 5.0 rating isn't shown on the site

You're rated 5.0 on Angi — that's real trust and it's currently invisible on your own homepage. Putting it front and center (and linking to the reviews) lifts calls from people already on the fence.
